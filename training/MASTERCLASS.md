## Training Methodology

### Hands-On Labs
Each module includes:
- **Theory**: Why this exists
- **Practice**: Do it yourself
- **Pressure Test**: Simulated incidents
- **Review**: What you learned

### Doctrine Immersion
- Week 1-2: Pure Velocity (no Phoenix)
- Week 3-4: Velocity with Phoenix awareness
- Week 5-6: Multi-doctrine systems
- Week 7-8: Doctrine creation

### Assessment Criteria

**Apprentice → Operator**
- Can execute under normal conditions
- Understands audit exists
- Knows when to ask for help

**Operator → Architect**
- Can design Velocity systems
- Handles incidents independently
- Optimizes for speed

**Architect → Master**
- Owns doctrine decisions
- Trains others effectively
- Handles edge cases

**Master → Sovereign**
- Creates new doctrines
- Owns system evolution
- Makes strategic decisions

## The Velocity Mindset

### Core Beliefs
1. **Speed is a feature, not a bug**
2. **Trust skilled operators**
3. **Governance after incidents**
4. **Actions speak for themselves**

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- ❌ Adding friction "just in case"
- ❌ Requiring approval for everything
- ❌ Slowing down "for safety"
- ❌ Hiding audit completely (it must exist)

### When to Flip
Velocity is wrong when:
- Regulated industry
- Legal exposure high
- Audit expected
- Team doesn't trust each other

**Flip to Phoenix. Same engine. Same data. One command.**
